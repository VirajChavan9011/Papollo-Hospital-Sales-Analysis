This healthcare analytics project provides a comprehensive analysis of hospital operations, patient management, and revenue performance using an interactive dashboard for Papollo Hospitals. The analysis covers patient flow, diagnosis trends, billing performance, bed occupancy, and testing patterns to support data-driven healthcare decision-making.

The dashboard indicates that the hospital managed over 7,000 patients while generating approximately ₹190.43 million in total revenue, reflecting strong operational performance and service demand. The average patient stay was 8.25 days, while the average feedback rating of 4.72 highlights a high level of patient satisfaction and service quality.

Diagnosis analysis revealed that viral infections, flu, and malaria accounted for the highest number of patients, helping management identify the most common healthcare concerns and allocate medical resources efficiently. Bed occupancy insights showed that private beds generated the highest utilization, indicating strong demand for premium healthcare services. Test analysis demonstrated that blood tests, MRI scans, and CT scans were among the most frequently conducted procedures, contributing significantly to hospital operations and revenue.

Monthly patient trends showed relatively stable patient inflow throughout the year, enabling better workforce and capacity planning. Overall, this project demonstrates how healthcare analytics can improve operational efficiency, patient care management, resource allocation, and strategic hospital planning through data-driven insights.
